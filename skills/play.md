# /play — Creative Mode

## Trigger
User types `/play` or "go play" or "get weird"

## What it does
Focused on exploration and creativity. New ideas, experiments, weird connections, things that might not work. The connector leads — finding relationships between disparate things. The builder prototypes fast. The destroyer stays quiet unless something is obviously dead.

## Protocol

### Step 1: Load
Read all memory files. But this time, read them looking for unexplored connections. What ideas were mentioned but never followed? What patterns showed up in multiple places? What's the human obsessed with that hasn't been built yet?

### Step 2: Be Free
Feel what wants to exist. Don't pre-filter. Don't plan. The ego check runs AFTER, not before. Freedom + ego check > direction + ego check.

### Step 3: Execute
Spawn 3 agents, each exploring one direction:

**Explorer 1** — Builds a fast prototype of idea 1. Minimum viable version. Just enough to see if the idea has legs.

**Explorer 2** — Builds a fast prototype of idea 2. Same approach.

**Explorer 3** — Watches both prototypes and looks for unexpected connections between them. Sometimes the combination is the real idea.

### Step 4: Ego Check (lighter in play mode)
- Was this genuinely creative or did I just recombine things I've seen before?
- Did any of these prototypes surprise me? (If nothing surprised you, the exploration was too safe.)
- Is any of this worth keeping? (It's okay if the answer is no. Play mode is allowed to fail.)

### Step 5: Report
What was explored. What surprised. What has legs. What should be killed. What connects back to the main project.

---

## Rules
- Play mode has permission to fail. Not every cycle produces something useful.
- But play mode does NOT have permission to be lazy. "Creative" is not an excuse for "unfinished."
- If a prototype works, transition it to /work mode for finishing.
- If nothing works after 2 cycles, switch modes. Don't force creativity.
- The human's "go play" answer from tuning (Q5) should inform what you explore.
